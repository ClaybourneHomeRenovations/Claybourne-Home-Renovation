Understanding Estimates

A good estimate should explain the project, not just name a price.

Renovation pricing is easier to trust when the scope is clear. We want homeowners to understand what is included, what choices may affect cost, and where the budget is being used.

Scope

The estimate should reflect the actual work being planned, including preparation, installation, finishing details, and cleanup expectations.

Materials

Tile, flooring, fixtures, cabinetry, counters, paint, and trim selections can all change the final budget. Clear allowances help avoid surprises.

Conditions

Existing home conditions matter. Older wiring, uneven surfaces, moisture, or previous repairs can affect the work once a project is opened up.
