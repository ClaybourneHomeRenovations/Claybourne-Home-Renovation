Material Costs

Understand what materials can do to your renovation budget.

Materials can dramatically change the final cost of a project. Flooring, tile, fixtures, cabinetry, paint, hardware, and finishes all affect the overall investment.

Choosing Smart Materials

The right materials balance style, durability, and cost.

Expensive materials are not always the best choice, and budget-friendly materials are not always poor quality. The key is choosing products that fit the room, the level of use, and the look you want.

For example, luxury vinyl plank can be a smart flooring option for busy homes because it is durable, modern, water-resistant, and more affordable than many hardwood options.

Tile, vanities, countertops, faucets, and lighting can all range from basic to premium. We help homeowners choose options that make sense for their goals instead of overspending where it does not matter.

Material tip:

Spend more on items that take daily wear, like flooring, faucets, and shower fixtures. Save on items that are easier to replace later, like mirrors, cabinet pulls, and décor.

What Affects Cost

Material prices are not one-size-fits-all.

The final cost depends on product quality, quantity, installation difficulty, availability, and whether extra preparation is needed.

Flooring

Vinyl, laminate, tile, and hardwood all have different price points. Subfloor condition and room shape can also affect labour and material waste.

Tile

Tile pricing depends on size, pattern, quality, waterproofing needs, grout, trim pieces, and installation complexity.

Fixtures

Faucets, toilets, vanities, lighting, and shower hardware can quickly change a bathroom or kitchen budget.
